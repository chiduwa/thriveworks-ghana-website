Our Impact

This is the story behind the numbers. ThriveWorks Ghana started because too many young people in Sunyani were left with nothing to do and nowhere to turn, and that kind of idle time can push someone toward alcohol, drugs, or despair. So we built something else: real training, real mentors, and a place to belong. Since 2021, that has grown into a program that's changed how 142 young people see their own future. Here's what that actually looks like, in their numbers and ours.

What “Digital Skills” Actually Means Here

This is our biggest track, and it isn't just typing lessons. We keep updating it so our apprentices leave knowing tools that are actually useful right now, not what was useful five years ago.

Core IT Literacy

Microsoft Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. The everyday tools behind office and admin work all over Ghana.

Design

Graphic design with Canva and Photoshop, building a portfolio our apprentices can use to freelance or land a design job.

AI-Assisted Creation

Hands-on practice with AI tools for sound and video editing and generation, so an idea can become finished work much faster.

“Vibe Coding”

A first taste of AI-assisted software development using tools like VS Code and Cursor. Apprentices build real, working software with AI as their coding partner.

Practical Foundations

Typing, internet research, email, and staying safe online. The basics that everything else here is built on.

Always Updating

We change this curriculum as the job market changes, so an apprentice who joins next year learns different tools than one who joined last year, on purpose.

Why We Stick Around for Years, Not Weeks

Learning a trade well takes time, especially through an apprenticeship. That's why our tracks run up to 3 years, with mentors staying close the whole way so character and discipline grow right alongside the technical skill.

How We Got Here

2021 to 2022

It started as an idea. We spent this time understanding what the community actually needed and building partnerships, including Urban Promise International, who became our fiscal sponsor.

2023

Incorporated ThriveWorks as an NGO in Ghana. Piloted and refined the program.

2024–2025

Program review and expansion to new sectors, doubling the number of youth served.

2026

Focused on securing funding for our training facility, expanding partnerships, strengthening staff, and increasing youth served through improved systems.
