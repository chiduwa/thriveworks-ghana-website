What “Digital Skills” Means at ThriveWorks

Our largest track isn't just computer basics — it's a deliberately forward-looking curriculum designed to keep our youth relevant in a fast-changing job market.

Core IT Literacy

Microsoft Office Suite — Word, Excel, and PowerPoint — the everyday tools of office and administrative work across Ghana.

Design

Graphic design fundamentals using Canva and Adobe Photoshop, building a portfolio youth can use to freelance or join a design team.

AI-Assisted Creation

Hands-on discovery of AI tools for sound and video editing and generation — turning ideas into finished content faster.

“Vibe Coding”

An introduction to AI-assisted software development using tools like VS Code and Cursor — letting beginners build real, working software with AI as a coding partner.

Practical Foundations

Typing, internet research, email and online communication, and digital safety — the basics every other module builds on.

Forward-Looking

We continually refresh this curriculum so it reflects the tools the job market actually wants next, not just what was current when a youth enrolled.

Holistic, Long-Term Support

Real skill mastery — especially through apprenticeship — takes time. Our apprenticeship tracks run up to 3 years, paired with continued mentorship so character and discipline grow alongside technical ability.

Tools & Resources

Continued Mentorship

Monitoring & Evaluation

Entrepreneurship Support

For apprentices ready to start their own business, we provide training, funds, and resources to support viable, nascent ventures.

Our Journey

2021–2022

Ideation and community needs assessment. Established partnerships for program development and fundraising — notably Urban Promise International as fiscal sponsor.

2023

Incorporated ThriveWorks as an NGO in Ghana. Piloted and refined the program.

2024–2025

Program review and expansion to new sectors, doubling the number of youth served.

2026

Focused on securing funding for our training facility, expanding partnerships, strengthening staff, and increasing youth served through improved systems.
